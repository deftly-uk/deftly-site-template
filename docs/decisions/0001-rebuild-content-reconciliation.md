# ADR 0001: How site rebuilds reconcile content (protecting customer edits)

- **Status:** Accepted (not yet implemented)
- **Date:** 2026-06-26
- **Deciders:** Harry (product owner), with Claude
- **Context:** Follow-up to the PR #2 review (multi-tenant engine). One of the
  reviewer's points was that rebuilds could silently delete content.

---

## The problem

The engine builds a customer's site from a *spec* (generated by the CRM). Sites get
**rebuilt**: the CRM may regenerate the spec while we iterate on a preview, and a build
can re-run. Today, when a rebuild writes content (services, testimonials) it matches each
item **by its title**. That causes two problems:

1. **Renames and removals leave stale content behind.** If the spec renames "Boiler
   repair" to "Boiler servicing", the old item stays live next to the new one. If a
   service is dropped from the spec, it stays on the site.
2. **The obvious fix is dangerous.** The naive cure ("on rebuild, delete anything not in
   the new spec") would also delete anything the **customer** added or edited themselves
   in the admin panel. That is the loss of a paying customer's own work, and it breaks the
   Constitution's core promise that the customer owns and can edit everything (Articles I
   and II).

So a rebuild has to be able to tell apart **content the engine generated** from **content
the customer created or changed**, and only ever manage its own.

---

## Options considered

### Option 1: Never auto-delete (additive only)
Rebuilds add and update, but never remove. Renamed or dropped services linger until
someone deletes them by hand.
- **Pro:** simplest; zero data-loss risk.
- **Con:** leftover and duplicate content after a rename; manual tidy-up; the rebuild no
  longer faithfully reflects the spec.

### Option 2: Tag engine-generated content  ✅ chosen
Every row the engine generates carries a hidden marker recording its origin. Rebuilds
reconcile **only marked rows**: update the ones still in the spec, delete the marked ones
that have vanished from it. Customer-created rows are never marked, so are never touched.
The moment a customer edits a marked row, ownership flips to the customer and the engine
leaves it alone from then on.
- **Pro:** rebuilds stay faithful to the spec **and** customer work is fully protected.
- **Con:** needs a small database change (one hidden field plus a migration) and a little
  more reconcile logic.

### Option 3: Spec is used once, at first build only
The engine builds the site once; after that the admin panel is the sole source of truth
and rebuilds do nothing destructive.
- **Pro:** simple, safe, philosophically clean.
- **Con:** spec tweaks made *before* launch do not fully reflect on rebuild (removals are
  ignored); less flexible during the preview-and-iterate phase.

---

## Decision

**Adopt Option 2 (tag engine-generated content).**

## Why

- It is the only option that keeps rebuilds faithful to the spec **without ever** risking
  the customer's own edits.
- It matches how the product actually works: we iterate on the spec and preview, then hand
  the site over to the customer, who owns it through the admin panel.
- The cost (one hidden field plus a migration) is small and one-time. The alternative
  failure (silently deleting a paying customer's content) is the worst outcome this
  product can have, so paying a little structure to prevent it is clearly worth it.

## Consequences (what implementing this involves)

> Scope note (after Codex review, 2026-06-26): this applies to **all** tenant content,
> including the per-tenant globals `site-settings` and `home-page` (which a rebuild also
> rewrites), not just the two list collections. The `origin`/`specKey` fields must be
> protected server-side (not just hidden in the admin UI), and the rebuild must be
> transactional and serialized per tenant. Full detail and the decided migration/backfill
> policy live in the execution plan
> (`docs/plans/2026-06-26-rebuild-content-reconciliation.md`, rev 2).

- A hidden **origin** field on the spec-managed collections (`services`, `testimonials`,
  `site-settings`, `home-page`, and any future spec-driven content), recording `spec` vs
  `owner`. Ideally also a **stable spec key** per list item so a rename updates the same
  row instead of creating a duplicate.
- A **reconcile step** on rebuild: upsert by the stable key, delete `spec`-managed rows
  that are absent from the new spec, and never touch `owner` rows.
- **Editing a spec-managed row in the admin promotes it to `owner`** (the engine stops
  overwriting it thereafter).
- A **database migration** and **tests** covering: rename, removal, customer-added item,
  and customer-edits-then-rebuild.
- **Until this is implemented, rebuilds are effectively additive** (the current behaviour)
  and should not be run against a launched site without care.
